## Anthropic's discussions with the US Dept. of War

Earlier this year, the US Department of War (DOW) issued a directive requiring all AI contractors to allow use of their models for "all lawful purposes"
without any vendor-imposed restrictions. While OpenAI and xAI reached agreements, Anthropic's CEO Dario Amodei refused, citing "democratic values" and
safety concerns. The red lines in question imposed by Anthropic were forbidding use of Claude for mass domestic surveillance as well as fully autonomous
weapons by the military. In an [open statement](http://anthropic.com/dow), Amodei explains that the company "cannot in good conscience" comply with
the government's demand, also also pointing out the irony of the government's threats being contradictory. Amodei concluded by stating that the company
would work to ensure a smooth transition to different providers.

We have also already found out that OpenAI is that different provider. [OpenAI's blog post](https://openai.com/index/our-agreement-with-the-department-of-war/)
