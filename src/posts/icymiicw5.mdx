## Spotify sues Anna's Archive

Following Anna's Archive (AA) Spotify scrape of nearly 300TB in songs, Spotify and 3 major labels have gone forward
with a 13 trillion dollar lawsuit against the shadow library. They allege AA scraped 86 million music files. As
expected, AA has not commented publicly on the lawsuit.

Read more at [NME](https://www.nme.com/news/music/spotify-major-record-labels-sue-annas-archive-13trillion-allege-brazen-theft-millions-files-3925977)

## Tiktok censorship in the US

Following the american acquisition of Tiktok's US operations, users have reported suspicions malfuntions. Controversial
users being shadowbanned, certain keywords can't be posted, such as "epstein", and videos of controversial topics
remaining "under review". Tiktok USDS has denied that such happenings are politically motivated, blaming a power
outage in an Oracle datacenter. California's governor, Gavin Newson, has called for a review into the new algorithm.

Read more at [The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2026/jan/30/tiktok-us-takeover-new-type-of-censorship)

## Age verified VPNs

After the UK's age restrictions laws went into effect, protecting children from shady websites, adults were enraged
that they had to verify with their ID everywhere, many of them preffering to use VPNs instead (including children).
Now, the UK House of Lords has voted on extending the age verification to VPNs and a wide range of online platforms.

You can read about the rest of the post at [Reclaim The Net](https://reclaimthenet.org/uk-house-of-lords-votes-to-extend-age-verification-to-vpns)
