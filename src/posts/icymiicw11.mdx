## Microsoft gets tired of "Microslop"

Microsoft's increasing push of AI in everything they own has caused them to gain the beautiful "Microslop"
nickname everywhere on social media. Microslop's Discord people weren't too happy with their nickname so
they blocked it in the official Microslop Copilot Discord server. After various people bypassed the block,
the server was completely locked. (lolz) For an update on the lockdown, read [Windows Latest's post](https://www.windowslatest.com/2026/03/02/microsoft-gets-tired-of-microslop-bans-the-word-on-its-discord-then-locks-the-server-after-backlash/)

## corporate jargon might make you dumb

Some very cool people at Cornell did some research on the usage of corporate jargon and they revelaed that people that use
such corporate-speak may struggle with decision-making. Shane Littrell works with the "Corporate Bullshit Receptivity Scale",
a tool to "measure susceptibility to impressive-but-empty organizational rhetoric". While I couldn't read the whole thing,
I had a great time with what I got. Check it out [here](https://news.cornell.edu/stories/2026/03/workers-who-love-synergizing-paradigms-might-be-bad-their-jobs)
